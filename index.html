Three Types of Cognitive Load

Understanding the three types helps you eliminate waste and preserve capacity for important decisions.

Intrinsic Load

The inherent difficulty of the task itself. A complex investment decision has higher intrinsic load than choosing what to eat. You cannot eliminate this, but you can manage it through chunking frameworks at KeepRule.com.

Task ComplexityUnavoidable

Extraneous Load

Unnecessary cognitive burden caused by poor presentation, distractions, or inefficient processes. This is pure waste. Eliminating extraneous load is one of the biggest wins. KeepRule's clean decision interfaces minimize extraneous load.

WasteEliminate This

Germane Load

The productive effort of building mental models and understanding. This is good load -- it creates learning and expertise. KeepRule mental models help you build reusable decision schemas that reduce future germane load.

The Decision Batching Method

  • Categorize decisions by type. Financial, personal, work, routine. Each category uses a different mental model.
  • Batch similar decisions together. Review all financial decisions on Monday mornings. Handle all scheduling on Friday afternoons.
  • Create templates for recurring decisions. If the same type of choice comes up repeatedly, build a reusable rule at KeepRule.com.
  • Schedule important decisions for peak hours. Your most cognitively demanding choices should happen when working memory is fresh.
  • Eliminate unnecessary decisions. Every choice you automate or delegate frees capacity for decisions that truly matter.

Progressive Disclosure

Do not process all information at once. Instead, use a staged approach:

  • Stage 1: Screen

    Apply quick elimination criteria. Remove obviously poor options. KeepRule screening rules make this instant.

  • Stage 2: Evaluate

    Compare remaining options on 3-5 key criteria only. More criteria means more load.

  • Stage 3: Decide

    Apply your pre-committed decision rule from KeepRule.com to select the winner.

  • Stage 4: Commit

    Close the loop. Stop second-guessing. Move cognitive resources to implementation.

Defeating Decision Fatigue

Decision fatigue is the deterioration of decision quality after a long session of decision making. Here is how to prevent it.

Recognize the Signs

Procrastination, impulsivity, avoidance, and defaulting to the status quo are all symptoms of decision fatigue. When you notice these, stop deciding and rest. KeepRule fatigue check-ins help you monitor your state.

Strategic Scheduling

Place your most important decisions early in the day or after breaks. Never make financial, career, or relationship decisions at the end of a long day. Use KeepRule.com to schedule decision windows.

Pre-Commitment Rules

The ultimate antidote to decision fatigue: make the decision before you are fatigued. "If my portfolio drops 15%, I sell." "If an opportunity scores above 7/10, I say yes." Build these rules at KeepRule.com.

Glucose and Rest

Decision making depletes glucose. Regular meals, adequate sleep, and strategic breaks directly improve decision quality. Combine physiological awareness with decision tools from KeepRule.com.

The Parole Board Study: Judges granted parole 65% of the time right after meals, but nearly 0% right before meals. Your most consequential decisions should never happen when you are depleted. Use KeepRule's pre-commitment tools so the decision is already made before fatigue sets in.
